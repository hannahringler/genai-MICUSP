**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement**

*   **Depth of Critique:** While the paper identifies shortcomings of current regulations (budget constraints, unclear language, poorly informed property owners), it could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the specific ways in which these limitations hinder effective lakefront management.
*   **Feasibility of Recommendations:** While the recommendations are well-defined, the paper could strengthen them by addressing potential challenges or obstacles to their implementation. For instance, how would the mandatory lecture for dock permits be enforced? What are the political or economic barriers to adopting the Alternative Shoreline Standards statewide?
*   **Clarity and Conciseness:** In a few places, the writing could be more concise. Some sentences or paragraphs are a bit lengthy and could be broken down for improved readability.
*   **Visual Aids Could Be Improved:** The figures (especially Figure 2) need improved formatting to make them legible.
*   **Limited Discussion of Social Equity:** The paper primarily focuses on ecological and economic aspects. It could be strengthened by briefly considering potential social equity implications of the proposed recommendations. For example, could increased permit fees disproportionately affect lower-income property owners?

**Specific Suggestions**

1.  **Elaborate on Critique:**
    *   Provide specific examples of "unclear language" in the regulations and how it leads to misinterpretation or ineffective enforcement.
    *   Explain in more detail how budget constraints limit the MDNR's ability to monitor and enforce regulations.
    *   Provide data, anecdotal or statistical, about how property owners are not informed about proper vegetation removal techniques.
2.  **Strengthen Recommendation Feasibility:**
    *   For the mandatory dock permit lecture, consider:  How will it be funded?  How long will it be?  How will attendance be tracked and enforced?
    *   For Alternative Shoreline Standards: Are there political interests that resist this? What costs of adopting these standards will be necessary?
    *   Address potential concerns about the increased costs and regulations by highlighting the long-term economic and ecological benefits of a sustainable lakefront.
3.  **Refine Writing:**
    *   Review the paper for lengthy sentences and paragraphs. Break them down into shorter, more manageable units.
    *   Use more active voice where appropriate to make the writing more direct and engaging.
4.  **Improve Visual Aids:**
    *   Figure 2 is especially difficult to read, and it needs clearer labeling.
    *   Consider using larger font sizes and clearer legends.
    *   Make sure all figures are properly cited.
5.  **Address Social Equity (Briefly):**
    *   Acknowledge the potential for increased permit fees to disproportionately affect lower-income property owners.
    *   Suggest potential solutions, such as a tiered fee structure or subsidies for low-income owners.
6.  **Conclusion:**
    *   Consider adding a brief concluding paragraph to summarize the main points of the paper and reiterate the importance of addressing the lakefront development problem in Minnesota.
